CGSC Hurricane Rules and Procedures

  • The Vice Commodore is responsible for Hurricane Procedures, and their execution. If the Vice Commodore is unavailable, the Commodore is responsible. If the Commodore is unavailable, the Rear Commodore is responsible.
  • The staff will execute the Hurricane Procedures, under the leadership of the General Manager and the various department heads.
  • Other club members, including bridge and board members, must support but not interfere with the execution of Hurricane Procedures. All questions and suggestions should be addressed to the Vice Commodore and General Manager.
  • Club staff are not available for hire to help members once the club has commenced Preparation for the Storm. Staff are employed by the club, and must work exclusively for the club.

July (before the start of the Hurricane Season)

CGSC Staff

  • General Manager to send out Hurricane Preparation instructions for Members.
    This is the email template to use.
  • Dock and maintenance staff to ensure all CGSC boats are ready for an evacuation.
  • Dock and maintenance staff to ensure all CGSC trailers are in working order.
  • General Manager to ensure garage is booked for powerboats.
  • General Manager and Dock Master to ensure Dinner Key is booked for Beneteaus.
  • General Manager and Dock Master to confirm where club boats will go, and alternatives for those that might not be possible (e.g., if Coral Gables Waterway is full).

Vice Commodore

  • Arrange Hurricane Preparation seminars for mid-June and start of July.
  • With the Strip Chairman, compile a list of members are willing to tow and/or store club boats on the strip in the event of a storm. Provide their names and contact information to the General Manager.
  • Compile a list of members who are willing to tow Youth Program coach powerboats to the parking garage (or other locations if necessary). Provide their names and contact information to the General Manager.
  • Compile a list of members who are willing to move club equipment (dock boxes, fuel containers, power tools, generators, etc.) to the safe storage space Provide their names and contact information to the General Manager.

After the Storm

CGSC Staff

  • Assess damage to club.
  • General Manager contacts members who have volunteered to tow/move/store boats/equipment, to schedule their help.
  • Dock or Maintenance Staff or volunteer member drives box truck from garage or safe place.
  • Dock Staff start club launch service.
  • Dock Staff start shuttle service from to Coral Gables Waterway from CGSC, and to Marine Stadium.
  • All Staff start After the Storm
  • General Manager annouces that the Crows Nest is opened. Bar Staff bring all tables and chairs on to the balcony from the bar.
  • General Manager to identify boats that violated the moorings or strip/rack agreement, and send violation notices signed by the Vice Commodore.

Vice Commodore

  • Assess damage to club, and convey to General Manager.
  • Identify boats that violated the moorings or strip/rack agreement, and convey to the General Manager for violation notices.
  • Recruit members to repair damage
    • Ensure toilets are working
    • Arrange food and drink with Bar Staff
    • Ask members to bring tools and fuel
