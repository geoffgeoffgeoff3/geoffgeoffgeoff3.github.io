OPEN WATER

Our Open Water Certification Class is taught by Charlie Matthews. Charlie Matthews is an SSI Instructor Trainer and has spent years working with the University of Miami Scuba Club.


The Course is broken down into three components:


  1. 1.Academics: This section consists of short lectures and reading assignments that cover SCUBA techniques, science, equipment and the underwater environment.  Lectures are two hours each twice a week for two weeks (either Monday/Wednesday or Tuesday/Thursday) .

  2. 2.Pool Training: Pool training involves learning how to assemble and use SCUBA gear. Pool sessions meet on weekends during the course.

  3. 3.Open Water Dives: Each dive is $55!! This is where you finally get to put into use everything you learned in the class by diving the Florida’s famous coral reefs. On the last weekend of the class you will go out and get in two days of diving. These trips are done in various south Florida dive locations.


Cost: The Open Water Certification Class is offered to UM SCUBA Club members at a discounted rate of $220 plus boat fees of $110. Refunds are a possibility if you give us written notice at least a week in advance. This cost includes instruction, all course materials, and equipment rentals for pool and Open Water Dives. Masks and fins are NOT included (as a club member you’ll need to have your own), and the boat charter cost for the two Open Water dives ($55 per day). After completing the course you will be able to start attending all UM Scuba Club trips up to a maximum depth of 60 ft.


If you have any questions about the certification class please feel free to contact umscuba@gmail.com or contact Charlie Matthews directly at 1-305-798-5144.


ADVANCED OPEN WATER

The Advanced Open Water course is the next step in diving education. Our Advanced Open Water Certification Class is taught by Jordan Harvell. Jordan is an SSI Instructor and has been working with the UM Scuba Club for the past 4 years alongside Charlie Matthews.


The AOW course is made up of four specialty certifications. These specialties include: Night and Limited Visibility, Deep Diver, Navigation Diver, and Nitrox.       


The course costs $220 and includes all books and materials. Boat charter fees are not included and each cost $55. At least two trips are required to complete the course. After completing the course you will be able to attend all UM Scuba Club trips, including those to sites deeper than 60 ft. If you have any questions please feel free to contact umscuba@gmail.com or Jordan Harvell directly at 1-305-746-4334.



NITROX

Enriched Air, EANx, or Nitrox all mean the same thing -- a gas mix with a higher amount of oxygen so you can stay down longer and have a shorter “ degassing” period. The course is composed of just one lecture (no pool sessions) but is one of the best certifications to get to expand your diving.




EMERGENCY CARE TRAINING


UM Scuba is now pleased to offer emergency care training courses to club members due to the generous support of the University of Miami Citizen’s Board.


Even the safest divers can find themselves involved with a diving accident, so having a basic understanding of CPR, first aid, and oxygen administration are critical skills all divers should possess.



The following courses may be offered throughout the semester :

DAN Basic Life Support: CPR and First Aid will teach you how to perform CPR, use

        an automated external defibrillator (AED), clear and obstructed airway, and

        introduce you to many common first aid skills.


DAN Emergency Oxygen for Scuba Diving Injuries will teach you the technique of

        emergency oxygen administration for suspected diving injuries and nonfatal

        drowning. You will learn the fundamentals of recognizing dive-injuries along with

        response and management. 


DAN First Aid for Hazardous Marine Life Injuries teaches divers how to identify and   

        avoid potentially harmful marine life. It also provides first aid skills to minimize

        these injuries and reduce discomfort.


DAN Neurological Assessment course focuses on how to obtain essential information

        about an individual involved in a dive emergency or suspected of having a stroke

        and what information to relay to EMS.


DAN Diving Emergency Management Provider The DEMP program prepares you to

        address multiple problems at once and with confidence. It combines the

       knowledge and skills from the following four courses into a single approach to

       managing diving emergencies: Basic Life Support: CPR and First Aid, Neurological   

       Assessment, Emergency Oxygen for Scuba Diving Injuries and First Aid for

       Hazardous Marine Life Injuries.

American Heart Association (AHA)

        Classes may be offered including Heartsaver CPR/First Aid/AED for Adult/Child/

        Infant, Bloodborne Pathogens and BLS for Healthcare Provider if requested.

RESCUE

“Challenging” and “rewarding” best describe the PADI Rescue Diver course. Building upon what you’ve already learned, this course expands on how to prevent problems and manage them if they occur.


Skills Learned:

  1. 1.Self rescue

  2. 2.Recognizing and managing stress in other divers

  3. 3.Emergency management and equipment

  4. 4.Rescuing panicked divers

  5. 5.Rescuing unresponsive divers

Estimated cost for this course is $220.

Rescue is a strongly encouraged course.



CAVERN

This is a challenging and very exciting course that includes four training dives over at least two days. During your first open water dive you'll practice line handling, reel use and emergency procedures without entering a cavern. But for your next three dives, you're  head
ed into the cavern, staying within the light zone and 40 meters/130 feet total distance from the surface. You will learn about:

  1. •Cavern navigation and line protocols

  2. •Planning, organization, techniques, problems and hazards of cavern diving

  3. •Special equipment use, such as lights, guidelines, reels and redundant breathing systems

  4. •Air sharing, disorientation, silting, line problems and other emergency procedures specific to cavern diving

  5. •Silt prevention, buoyancy control, air management and emergency procedures

  6. •Depth and distant limits for cavern diving


The line drills will be done in Miami and the dives will be on our annual camping trip to Ginnie Springs. cost varies but the minimum would be $220. You must be at least Advanced Open Water.



DIVEMASTER

During the PADI Dive master program, you learn dive leadership skills through both
classroom and independent study. You will hone your in-water SCUBA and rescue skills and stamina, as well as become proficient in calmly and efficiently solving problems. Additionally, you will learn how to help others improve their SCUBA skills.


You put this knowled
ge into action through a structured internship or series of practical training sessions. As you progress through your Divemaster course, you’ll expand your diving knowledge, master SCUBA skills, and increase your confidence. Then, as a PADI Divemaster, you’ll to lead, mentor, and motivate other divers and see your hard work translate into their own enjoyment of the underwater world.
