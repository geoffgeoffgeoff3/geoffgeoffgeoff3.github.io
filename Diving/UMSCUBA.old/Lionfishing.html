Lionfish are an Indo-Pacific fish invasive to Florida waters. They eat everything, live everywhere, and have no natural predators, making them a very bad guy to have around. Because we dive so often, we thought we could help to make a dent in the population and protect our natural reefs.


Who in the club can go Lionfishing?

  1. •Members who have attended a REEF lionfish workshop or Biscayne National Park lionfish workshop

  2. •Members who have been certified by a club officer to have proficient dive skills, good buoyancy, and are competent at using a pole spear.

  3. •Members who have been awarded a “UM SCUBA Lionfish membership card”


What’s the procedure on each dive?

On club dives allowing lionfishing, we have a limit of four spears (total of 8 people or 4 buddy teams).

For each buddy team spearing, one person will be assigned a zookeeper (a container that holds already-speared fish). Why? To reduce the risk of people spearing each other and make it easier to safely store the fish.

All lionfishing must be done under the supervsion of a club officer.


What equipment is provided by the club?

  1. •Polespear

  2. •Gloves (relatively heavy duty)

  3. •Zookeeper

  4. •Cooler (for transport on boat)


Guidelines for hunting:

  1. •Be aware of your surroundings

  2. •Make sure you are not pointing the spear at any other divers.

  3. •Do not cause damage to any coral that you may hit in the process of spearing the lionfish. Work with the best angle.

  4. •Be aware of the wildlife around, including sharks which may be attracted by a kill.

  5. •DO NOT keep the spear loaded all the time.

  6. •Only load the spear when you are ready to shoot.

  7. •When possible, aim the spear tip at the head of the fish. Once alerted of a threat, the lionfish will try to swim forward.

  8. •Be conscious of your buoyancy and your breathing (continue breathing deeply and slowly, you are still on SCUBA gear!)

  9. •Do not remove the spines of the lion fish on the boat -- wait until you get back to solid ground.


Spearfishing regulations:

You may NOT spearfish as described below (FL rules, sorry!):


  1. •Spearfishing of marine and freshwater species in freshwater is prohibited. Possession of a spear gun in or on freshwater is also prohibited.

  2. •Within 100 yards of a public swimming beach, any commercial or public fishing pier, or any part of a bridge from which public fishing is allowed.

  3. •Within 100 feet of any part of a jetty that is above the surface of the sea - except for the last 500 yards of a jetty that extends more than 1,500 yards from the shoreline.

  4. •In Monroe County from Long Key north to the Dade County line.

  5. •For any fish for which spearing is expressly prohibited by law (listed above).

  6. •In any body of water under the jurisdiction of the Environmental Protection, Recreation and Parks.  (Possession of spearfishing equipment is prohibited in these areas, unless it is unloaded and properly stored.)

  7. •Fishermen who catch and/or sell fish harvested by spearing are subject to the same rules and limitations that other anglers in the state are required to follow.


More regulations can be found at http://www.myfwc.com/fishing/saltwater/recreational/spearing/
